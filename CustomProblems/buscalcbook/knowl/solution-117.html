Solution 5.7.c.1.

The future value function is

\begin{equation*} V = P \cdot (1-0.3)^t \end{equation*}

We want the time \(t\) when the value reaches \(V=100\) when from a starting value of \(P=1,000\text{.}\) Plugging this in gives

\begin{align*} 100 \amp = 1000 \cdot (0.7)^t\\ 0.1 \amp = (0.7)^t\\ \ln\Big(0.1\Big) \amp = \ln\Big((0.7)^t\Big)\\ \ln\Big(0.1\Big) \amp = t\cdot \ln\Big(0.7\Big)\\ t \amp = \dfrac{ \ln\Big(0.1\Big)}{\ln\Big(0.7\Big)} = 6.45\dots \end{align*}

It will take about 6.45 years for the balance to decline from 1000 to 100.
