  1. Solve the inequality \(f(x) = 0\text{.}\)

  2. Draw a number line, and label the \(x\)-values where the solutions occur.

  3. Notice that you have divided the \(x\)-axis into several different intervals. Now, look at each interval separately.

    For each interval, use a graph, a calculator, or some other technique to decide whether the inequality is true or false on that interval.

  4. Write your final answer down in interval notation, excluding the endpoints (using parentheses).
