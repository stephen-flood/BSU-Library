To answer these questions clearly and concisely, we need new mathematical language. To understand our notation, it will be helpful to remember the number line. If you (\(x\)) approach a number \(a\) from the left, you are approaching \(a\) from the side of the number line with all the negative numbers. Because of that, we write \(x\rightarrow a^-\) as a shorthand for approaches \(a\) from the left.

If you (\(x\)) approach a number \(a\) from the right, you are approaching \(a\) from the side of the number line with all the positive numbers. Because of that, we write \(x\rightarrow a^+\) as a shorthand for approaches \(a\) from the right.
