Suppose that \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) are differentiable functions. Then

\begin{equation*} \ddx{f(x)\cdot g(x)} = f\cdot \ddx{g(x)} + g(x) \cdot \ddx{f(x)} \end{equation*}

This is eaiser to remember in bracket notation:

\begin{equation*} \Big[f\cdot g\Big]' = f\cdot \Big[g\Big]' + g\cdot \Big[f\Big]' = f\cdot g' + g\cdot f' \end{equation*}
