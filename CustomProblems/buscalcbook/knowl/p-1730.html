The finding the antiderivative of \(f''\) gives

\begin{align*} \amp = 6\cdot(\frac{1}{2} x^2) + \frac{1}{-1}x^{-1} + C\\ \amp = 3 x^2 - \frac{1}{x} + C \end{align*}

We are also given that \(f'(1)=4\text{.}\) Plugging \(1\) into the above equation gives

\begin{align*} f'(1)= 4 = \amp 3(1)^2 - \frac{1}{1} + C\\ 4 = \amp 3 - 1 + C\\ 2 = \amp C \end{align*}

Plugging this into our equation for \(f'\) gives

\begin{align*} f'(x) \amp = 3 x^2 - \frac{1}{x} + 2 \end{align*}
