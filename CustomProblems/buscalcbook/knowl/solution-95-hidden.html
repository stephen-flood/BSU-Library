Start by writing down the formula for DefinitionÂ 4.12.

\begin{equation*} F(t) = P \cdot \left(1 + \frac{r}{m}\right)^{m\cdot t} \end{equation*}

Using the principal \(P=100\text{,}\) decimal rate \(r=0.2\text{,}\) and frequency of compounding \(m=4\text{,}\) since there are four quarters in each year.

That gives the equation

\begin{equation*} F(t) = 100 \cdot \left(1 + \frac{0.2}{4}\right)^{4\cdot t} \end{equation*}

We could stop there, but it is often nice to clean this up by simplifying the base of the exponent to get a formula for the value after \(t\) years

\begin{equation*} F(t) = 100 \cdot \left(1.05 \right)^{4\cdot t} \end{equation*}

To find the value after one year, plug in \(t=1\) and simplify,

\begin{equation*} F(1) = 100 \cdot \left(1.05 \right)^{4\cdot 1} = \dots = 100\cdot 1.2155 = 121.55 \end{equation*}
