Solution 2.5.a.1.

We are given two points on the line. When the quantity is \(x_1=15\text{,}\) the price is \(p_1=4\text{.}\) When the quantity is \(x_2=20\text{,}\) the price is \(p_2=2\text{.}\)

To find the equation for the line, use the point-slope form

\begin{equation*} y = {\color{green} m} (x - { \color{green} x_1}) + {\color{green} y_1} \text{.} \end{equation*}

For the slope of the line, we use

\begin{align*} m \amp = \dfrac{\Delta p}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{p_2-p_1}{x_2-x_1}\\ \amp = \dfrac{2-4}{20-15}\\ \amp = \dfrac{-2}{5}=-0.4 \end{align*}

Putting it all together, we get

\begin{equation*} p(x) = -0.4 (x - 15) + 4 \end{equation*}

Simplifying this, we get

\begin{equation*} p(x) = -0.4x + 10 \end{equation*}
