Definition 4.5. Percent Change.

Suppose that \(r\) is a decimal rate, and that \(P\) is any value. If you increase \(P\) by the rate \(r\) you get the expression \(P+rP\text{,}\) also written

\begin{equation*} P\cdot (1+r)\text{.} \end{equation*}

To decrease \(P\) by the rate of \(m\), subtract \(rP\) instead, resulting in the expression

\begin{equation*} P\cdot (1-r) \end{equation*}
