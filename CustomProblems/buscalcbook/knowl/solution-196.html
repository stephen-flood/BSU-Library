Solution 12.4.b.1.

Using the sign chart for \(f'\text{,}\) we see that there is a local minima at \(x=-3\text{,}\) and there is no local maxima.

Aside: Note that the something very strange happens at \(x=-2\text{.}\) With a little more information about the original function, we would learn that \(f\) is undefined at \(x=-2\) and therefore could not be an extrema regardless of the sign chart. However, these details are beyond the scope of this course, and won't come up in any of our examples.
