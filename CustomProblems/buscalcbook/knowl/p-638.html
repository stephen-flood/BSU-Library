How much will the value be after two years? In the second year, the value of \(105\) increases a second time, also by 5%, or a rate of \(r=0.05\text{.}\) That means

\begin{equation*} V(2) = 105 + 105\cdot 0.05 = 110.25 \end{equation*}

In our notation for percent change, we can write this as

\begin{equation*} V(2) = 105\cdot (1+0.05) = 105\cdot (1.05) \end{equation*}

But the leading coefficent of \(105\) can also be rewritten, since \(105 = 100\cdot (1.05)\text{.}\) In other words,

\begin{equation*} V(2) = 100 \cdot (1.05) \cdot (1.05) = 100\cdot (1.05)^2 \end{equation*}
