To find the equation of a linear price function, use the point-slope form of the line

\begin{equation*} p(x) = m (x-x_1) + p_1 \end{equation*}

Note that we are told that \(x_1=100\) items are sold when the item price is \(p_1=12\text{,}\) and that \(x_2=500\) items are sold when the price is \(p_2=4\text{.}\)

That gives us a slope

\begin{equation*} m = \dfrac{\Delta p}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{p_2-p_1}{x_2-x_1} = \dfrac{4-12}{500-100} = \dfrac{-8}{400} = -0.02 \end{equation*}

Putting it all together we get

\begin{equation*} p(x) = -0.02 (x-100) + 12 = \dots = -0.02x + 14 \end{equation*}
