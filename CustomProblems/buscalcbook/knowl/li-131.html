Item 1.

Near a point \((a,f(a))\text{,}\) the function \(f(x)\) is approximately equal to the line tangent to \(f\) at that point. Mathematically, this means

\begin{equation*} (\text{height on function }f) \approx (\text{height on tangent line centered at } a) \end{equation*}

for all values of \(x\) near to \(a\)
