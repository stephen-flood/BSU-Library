25% corresponds to a decimal rate of \(r=\frac{25}{100} = 0.25\text{.}\) To increase $1000 by 25%, add $1000 to \(1000(0.25)\)

\begin{equation*} 1000 + 1000(0.25) = 1000 + 250 = 1250 \end{equation*}

We could also just have multiplied \(1000\) by \(1+0.25 = 1.25\)

30% corresponds to a decimal rate of \(r = 0.3\text{.}\) To decrease $1250 by 30%, take $1250 and subtract \(1250(0.3)\)

\begin{equation*} 1250 - 1250(0.3)=875 \end{equation*}

We could also just have multiplied \(1000\) by \(1 + (-0.3) = 0.7\)
