Theorem 12.6. The First Derivative Test.

Suppose that \(c\) is a critical number of a function \(f\text{.}\)

  1. If \(f'\) goes from \((+)\) to \((-)\) at \(c\) (\(f\) goes from \(\nearrow\) to \(\searrow\)) then \(f\) has a local maximum at \(c\text{.}\)

  2. If \(f'\) goes from \((-)\) to \((+)\) at \(c\) (\(f\) goes from \(\searrow\) to \(\nearrow\)) then \(f\) has a local minimum at \(c\text{.}\)

  3. If \(f'\) does not change sign at \(c\) (if \(f\) goes \(\searrow\,\searrow\) or \(\nearrow\,\nearrow\)), then \(f\) does not have a local maximum or minimum at \(c\text{.}\)
