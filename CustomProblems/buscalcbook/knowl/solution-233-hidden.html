We are given that \(C'(x) = 36x + \frac{42}{x} \)

Finding the (general) antiderivative gives total cost function

\begin{align*} C(x) = \amp 36\cdot \frac{1}{2}x^2 + 42\cdot \ln(x) + C\\ = \amp 18 x^2 + 42\ln(|x|) + C \end{align*}

We are also told that 1 item costs $200, which says \(C(1) = 200\text{.}\) Plugging 1 into the equation above gives

\begin{align*} C(1) = 200 = \amp 18(1)^2 + 42\ln(|1|) + C \\ 200 = \amp 18 + 0 + C \\ 182 = \amp C \end{align*}

Plugging this into the total cost function gives us that the total cost of \(x\) items is

\begin{equation*} C(x) = 18x^2 + 42\cdot \ln(|x|) + 182 \end{equation*}
