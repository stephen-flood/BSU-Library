Suppose that \(g(x)\) is a differentiable function, and that \(m\) is a constant number.

\begin{equation*} \ddx{ \Big( g(x) \Big)^m} = m\cdot \Big( g(x) \Big)^{m-1} \cdot \ddx{g(x)} \end{equation*}

In other words, if you have a composition \(h(x) = \Big(g(x)\Big)^m\text{,}\) the derivative is the same thing as following the power rule and multiplying by the derivative of the argument.
