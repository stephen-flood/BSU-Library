This function is the sum of three terms. Fortunately, the derivative of the sum is the sum of the derivatives. That means that we can separately find the antiderivative of each term.

From before,

  • An antiderivative of \(6x^2\) is

    \begin{equation*} 6\frac{1}{2+1}x^{2+1} = 6\cdot \frac{1}{3} x^3 \end{equation*}

  • An antiderivative of \(7e^x\) is

    \begin{equation*} 7e^x \end{equation*}

  • An antiderivative of \(-2\) is

    \begin{equation*} -2x \end{equation*}

Putting these together, the antiderivative of

\begin{equation*} f(x) = 6x^2 + 7e^x - 2 \end{equation*}

is equal to

\begin{align*} F(x) \amp = 6\cdot\frac{1}{3} x^3 + 7 e^x -2x + C\\ \amp = 2x^3 + 7x -2x + C \end{align*}
