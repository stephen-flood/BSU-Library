To find the equation of a linear function, use the point-slope form of the line

\begin{equation*} p(x) = m (x-x_1) + p_1 \end{equation*}

Note that we are told that \(x_1=100\) items are sold when the item price is \(p_1=11\text{,}\) and that \(x_2=200\) items are sold when the price is \(p_2=1\text{.}\)

That gives us a slope

\begin{equation*} m = \dfrac{\Delta p}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{p_2-p_1}{x_2-x_1} = \dfrac{1-11}{200-100} = \dfrac{-10}{100} = -0.1 \end{equation*}

Putting it all together we get

\begin{equation*} p(x) = -0.1 (x-100) + 11 = \dots = -0.1x + 21 \end{equation*}
