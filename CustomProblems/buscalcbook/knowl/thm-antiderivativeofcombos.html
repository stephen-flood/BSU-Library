Theorem 14.4.

  • Suppose that one antiderivative of \(f(x)\) is \(F(x)\) and that \(c\) is a constant number. Then one antiderivative of \(c\cdot f(x)\) is given by \(c\cdot F(x)\text{.}\)

  • Suppose that one antiderivative of \(f(x)\) is \(F(x)\) and that one antiderivaitve of \(g(x)\) is \(G(x)\text{.}\) Then one antiderivative of \(f(x)+g(x)\) is given by \(F(x) + G(x)\text{.}\)
