Average and Change look Backward

If you have moved from \(x_1\) to \(x_2\) items, then you can compute the change

\begin{equation*} \Delta C = C_2 - C_1 \end{equation*}

and the average rate (AROC)

\begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta C}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{C_2 - C_1}{x_2-x_1} \end{equation*}

Finding the exact cost of a single item is a special case of finding the change in cost.

For example, to find the exact cost of item 105, compute \(C(105)-C(104)\text{.}\) This can be thought of as a change \(\Delta C\text{,}\) or an average change \(\frac{\Delta C}{\Delta x}\) with \(\Delta x=1\)

Change and AROC are used for accounting for changes in the past.
