We are asked to take the antiderivative of \(x^{55}\text{.}\) We could do that using our methods from before (including guess/check/correct) to say that the antiderivative is \(F(x) = \frac{1}{56}x^{56}\)

Because \(55\neq -1\text{,}\) we can also use the indefinite integral formula \(\intdx{x^m} = \frac{1}{m+1}x^{m+1} + C\)

\begin{align*} \intdx{ x^{55} } \amp = \dfrac{1}{55+1} x^{55+1} \\ \amp = \dfrac{1}{56} x^{56} \end{align*}

In this example, the notation is the only difference between guess/check/correct and \(\intdx{\dots}\) notation. In more complex examples, \(\intdx{\dots}\) notation will be much more helpful.
