Definition 15.3.

Suppose that you are given any function \(y=f(x)\text{,}\) and two \(x\) values \(a,b\text{.}\) Let \(R\) represent the region enclosed between \(f\) and the \(x\)-axis on the interval \([a,b]\)

  1. The absolute area of the region \(R\) is the area you treat the region as a geometric figure, and ignore the calculus setting of the question.

  2. If the region \(R\) is entirely above the \(x\)-axis, then the region has positive net area equal to its absolute area.

  3. If the region \(R\) is entirely below the \(x\)-axis, the region has negative net area equal to the negation of its absoulte area.

  4. If the region \(R\) is sometimes above and sometimes below the \(x\)-axis, you must divide it into separate components, find their net area, and then add them together.
