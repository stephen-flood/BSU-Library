Definition 14.5. Indefinite Integral Notation.

If the general antiderivative of \(f(x)\) is \(F(x) + C\text{,}\) then we write

\begin{equation*} \intdx{ f(x) } = F(x) + C \end{equation*}

and say that the indefinite integral of \(f(x)\) is \(F(x)+C\)

For now, treat the expression \(\quad \red{\int}\, \blue{f(x)}\,\,\green{dx}\quad\) as an abbreviation for the sentence “take the antiderivative (\(\red{\int}\)) of \(\blue{f(x)}\) with respect to the variable \(x\) (\(\green{dx}\))”.
