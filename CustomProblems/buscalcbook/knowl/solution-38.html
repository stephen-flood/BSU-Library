Solution 2.5.c.1.

We want to find the quantity \(x\) for which the price \(p(x)=-0.4x+10\) is equal to \(8.4\text{.}\)

In other words, set the price function equal to \(8.4\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} 8.4 = p(x) = -0.4x + 10 \end{equation*}

Solve for \(x\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 8.4 \amp = \amp-0.4x \amp + 10\\ - 10 \amp = \amp \amp -10\\ \amp \amp \amp \\ -1.6 \amp = \amp -0.4x \amp \\ \div -0.4 \amp \amp \div -0.4\\ \amp \amp \amp \\ 4 \amp= \amp x \amp \end{align*}

Four items will be sold when each item costs \(8.4\) dollars.

Because Revenue equals price times quantity (\(R(x) = x \cdot p(x)\)),

\begin{equation*} R(x) = 4 \cdot 8.4 = 33.6 \end{equation*}

The total revenue will be $33.60.
