Units also help us make sense of equations once we've set them up. You can generally think of “units of measure” like variables, which means you can use algebra to interpret any equation.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \text{Total Cost} \quad = \quad \$ &\quad =\quad \big(\$\big)\quad +\quad \left( \text{item}\cdot\dfrac{\$}{\text{item}}\right) \\ &\quad =\quad \big(\$\big)\quad +\quad \left( {\color{red}\text{item}}\cdot\dfrac{\$}{{\color{red}\text{item}}}\right) \\ &\quad =\quad \$\quad + \quad \$ \\ &\quad =\quad \$\end{aligned} \end{equation*}
