It is common to talk about an economic quanity by changing by a given percentage.

The term percent is an abbreviation of the expression per cent, which means per one hundred. In other words, \(25\)% is the same thing as \(25\) hundreds, or a decimal rate of \(\dfrac{25}{100}=0.25\text{.}\)

For example, \(25\)% of \(1000\text{,}\) is equal to \(1000\cdot 0.25 = 250\text{.}\)

Now, suppose that you say that \(1000\) increases by \(25\) percent. How much do you have after this increase?

If you are increasing the value by 25%, you keep the original \(1000\text{,}\) and add the new \(250\text{.}\) In other words, you have

\begin{equation*} 1000 + 1000\cdot 0.25 = 1250\text{.} \end{equation*}

Suppose that you increase \(700\) by 25%. You take the original \(700\text{,}\) and add the \(700\cdot 0.25=175\) to get the final value

\begin{equation*} 700 + 700\cdot 0.25 = 875 \end{equation*}

There is a pattern here. If you take a principal value of \(P\text{,}\) and increase it by a decimal rate of \(r\text{,}\) the final value will be

\begin{equation*} P + P \cdot r \end{equation*}

We can factor a \(P\) out of each term, to get that the result of increasing \(P\) by a decimal rate of \(r\) results in a final value of

\begin{equation*} P\cdot (1+r) \end{equation*}

To decrease \(1000\) by \(25\)%, subtract \(0.25 \cdot 1000 = 250\) from \(1000\text{,}\) resulting in

\begin{equation*} 1000 - 1000\cdot 0.25 = 1000 - 250 = 750 \end{equation*}

To decrease a principal value of \(P\) by a decimal rate of \(r\text{,}\) compute

\begin{equation*} P - P\cdot r = P(1-r) \end{equation*}
