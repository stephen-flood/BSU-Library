Solution 3.1.b.1.

Remember that there are two ways to find the \(x\)-value of the vertex.

Because the vertex is the average of the roots, and because we have already found that the roots are \(x=1\) and \(x=2\text{,}\) the vertex has \(x\)-value

\begin{equation*} h=\dfrac{1+2}{2} = 1.5\text{.} \end{equation*}

On the other hand, for quadratics only, we have a nice formula, that \(h=\dfrac{-b}{2a} = \dfrac{-3}{2\cdot (-1)}= 1.5\)

To find the \(y\)-value of the vertex \(k\text{,}\) just compute \(k = f(h) \)

\begin{equation*} k = -(1.5)^2 + 3(1.5) - 2 = 0.25 \end{equation*}
