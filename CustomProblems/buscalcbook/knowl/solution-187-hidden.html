The graph has a local minimum at \(x=-2\text{,}\) \(x=1\text{,}\) or \(x=4\)

These are local minima because if you imagine standing on the graph at those points, moving left or right will increase your height.

Said another way, there is a local minima because when you read the graph from left to right, these are the \(x\)-values where the graph looks like \(\searrow\, \nearrow\text{.}\)
