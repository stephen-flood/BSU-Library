Recall DefinitionÂ 3.13. Write down the definition of profit

\begin{equation*} P(x) = R(x) - C(x) \end{equation*}

Next, replace \(R(x)\) with \((-x^2 + 5x)\) and \(C(x)\) with \((x+2)\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} P(x) = (-x^2 + 5x) - (x+2) \end{equation*}

Note that the parentheses are very important -- you must subtract both the \(x\) and the fixed cost \(2\text{.}\)

Finally, simplify the expression

\begin{align*} P(x) \amp = (-x^2 + 5x) - (x+2)\\ \amp = -x^2 + 5x -x -2\\ \amp = -x^2 + 4x -2 \end{align*}
