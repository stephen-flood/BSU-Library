\begin{equation*} f'(x) = \ddxfrac{ \dfrac{e^x}{x} } \end{equation*}

To apply our derivative rules, you need to use order of operations (PEMDAS) to identify the "outermost" operation. In this case, our function is the fraction of \(e^x\) and \(xx\text{.}\) That means we will use the quotient rule \(\left[\dfrac{\red{t}}{\blue{b}}\right]' = \dfrac{\blue{b}\cdot \red{t}' - \red{t}\cdot \blue{b}'}{(\blue{b})^2}\)

\begin{align*} f'(x) = \amp \ddxfrac{ \dfrac{\red{e^x}}{\blue{x}} }\\ = \amp \dfrac{\blue{x}\cdot \ddx{\red{e^x}} - \red{e^x} \ddx{\blue{x}}}{(\blue{x})^2}\\ = \amp \dfrac{ x\cdot \ddx{ e^x } - e^x \ddx{ x }}{(x)^2}\\ = \amp \dfrac{ x\cdot e^x - e^x \cdot 1}{(x)^2}\\ = \amp \dfrac{ x\cdot e^x - e^x }{(x)^2} \end{align*}

At \(x=10\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} f'(10) = \dfrac{ 10\cdot e^{10} - e^{10} }{(10)^2} = 1982.38 \end{equation*}
