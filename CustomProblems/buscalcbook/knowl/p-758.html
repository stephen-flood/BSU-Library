This is just a more complex way of asking us to simplify the natural log of e to various numbers. Reasoning as above gives

\begin{equation*} f(g(3)) = \ln(e^3) = 3 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(g(-2)) = \ln(e^{-2}) = -2 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(g(45)) = \ln(e^{45}) = 45 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(g(x)) = \ln(e^{x}) = x \end{equation*}
