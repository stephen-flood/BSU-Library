Solution 3.4.c.1.

Recall that \(p(600)\) is the item price at which \(x=600\) items will be sold, and \(R(600)\) will be the total revenue from that scenario.

In the scenario where \(x=600\) items are sold, \(p(600) = -0.02x + 14 = 2\text{.}\) In other words, each item will be sold for $\(2\)

In this scenario, the total revenue will be \(600\) items times \(2\) dollars, which equals $\(1200\) of total revenue.

Equivalently, \(R(600) = -0.02\cdot (600)^2 + 14 \cdot (600) = \dots = 1200\) dollars.
