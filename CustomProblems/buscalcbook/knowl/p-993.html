We will approximate the slope at a value of \(a\) by first drawing the tangent to the curve at that point. We can then find two points on the line and use the slope formula

\begin{equation*} m = \dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} \end{equation*}

Note that these points will not be on the original curve, since we are technically finding the slope of the tangent line, which we are only using to approximate the real curve.
