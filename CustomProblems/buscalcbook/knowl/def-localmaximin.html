Definition 12.3.

Let \(f\) be a function which is continuous at \(x=a\text{.}\)

  1. \(f\) has a local maxima at \(x=a\) if the graph goes from increasing to decreasing at \(a\) (the function looks like \(\nearrow\,\searrow\) at \(a\)).

  2. \(f\) has a local minima at \(x=a\) if the graph goes from decreasing to increasing at \(a\) (the function looks like \(\searrow\,\nearrow\) at \(a\)).

  3. \(f\) has an extrema at \(x=a\) if there is either a maxima or minima at \(x=a\text{.}\)
