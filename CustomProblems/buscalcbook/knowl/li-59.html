Solving by Factoring

We know that a product \(a\cdot b= 0\) if and only if \(a=0\) or \(b=0\text{.}\) So to solve the equation

\begin{equation*} -0.4x^2 + 10x = 0 \end{equation*}

since we have 0 on the right, we should try factoring the left side. Because both terms have an \(x\text{,}\) we can factor \(x\) out of both terms.

\begin{equation*} x\cdot (-0.4x + 10 ) = 0 \end{equation*}

This product can equal zero if and only if either

\begin{equation*} x=0 \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} -0.4x + 10 = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}

There are two quantities where revenue equals zero. The first point is \(x=0\)

To find the second point, solve the second equation for \(x\)

\begin{align*} -0.4x \amp + 10 \amp = \amp 0 \\ \amp - 10 \amp \amp - 10 \\ \amp \\ -0.4x \amp \amp = \amp -10 \\ \div -0.4 \amp \amp \div -0.4 \\ \amp \\ x \amp \amp = \amp 25 \end{align*}

The revenue equals zero when \(x=0\) or \(x=25\text{.}\)
