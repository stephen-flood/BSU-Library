There is a pattern here. If you take a principal value of \(P\text{,}\) and increase it by a decimal rate of \(r\text{,}\) the final value will be

\begin{equation*} P + P \cdot r \end{equation*}

We can factor a \(P\) out of each term, to get that the result of increasing \(P\) by a decimal rate of \(r\) results in a final value of

\begin{equation*} P\cdot (1+r) \end{equation*}
