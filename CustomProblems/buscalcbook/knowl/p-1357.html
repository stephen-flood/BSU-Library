To find when \(f'=0\) or \(f'\ DNE\text{,}\) we must first find an equation for the derivative \(f'\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} f'(x) = \amp \ddx{ \frac{-1}{3} x^3 + x + 1 } \\ = \amp \ddx{ \frac{-1}{3} x^3 } + \ddx{x} + \ddx{1} \\ = \amp \frac{-1}{3} \ddx{x^3} + 1 + 0\\ = \amp \frac{-1}{3}\cdot 3x^2 + + 1\\ = \amp -x^2 + 1 \end{align*}
