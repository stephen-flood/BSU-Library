Again, start by writing down the formula for Definition 4.12

\begin{equation*} F(t) = P \cdot \left(1 + \frac{r}{m}\right)^{m\cdot t}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Next, fill in the values given.

\begin{equation*} F(t) = P \cdot \left(1 + \frac{0.2}{4}\right)^{4\cdot t}\text{.} \end{equation*}

No other values are given in this part, so we now simplify.

\begin{equation*} F(t) = P \cdot \left(1.05\right)^{4\cdot t} \end{equation*}

Using the law of algebra \(a^{rs} = (a^r)^s\text{,}\) we get

\begin{align*} F(t) = \amp P \cdot \left(1.05^4\right)^{t}\\ = \amp P \cdot \left(1.2155\dots \right)^{t} \\ = \amp P \cdot \left(1 + 0.2155\dots \right)^{t} \end{align*}

Recall Proposition 4.7. This reminds us that the expression above coressponds to the process of repeatedly increasing the principal \(P\) by \(21.55\dots\)% each year.
