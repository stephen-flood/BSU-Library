Definition 8.11.

Let \(y=f(x)\) be any function.

Because \(f'(x) = \lim_{\Delta x\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\text{,}\) we often write

\begin{equation*} f'(x) = \dfrac{df}{dx} = \dfrac{dy}{dx} \end{equation*}

This notation helps us remember that

\begin{equation*} (\text{units of }f') = \dfrac{(\text{units of f})}{(\text{units of x})} \end{equation*}
