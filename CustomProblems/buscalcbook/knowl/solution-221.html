Solution 14.2.b.1.

To find the antiderivative of \(7\cdot\frac{1}{x}\text{,}\) there are three steps

  1. Find an antiderivative of \(\frac{1}{x}\)

  2. Multiply the antiderivative of \(\frac{1}{x}\) by 7

  3. Add \(+C\) to get the antiderivative.

This means that if \(f(x) = 7\frac{1}{x} \text{,}\) the antiderivative is

\begin{equation*} F(x) = 7\cdot \Big( \ln(|x|) \Big) + C \end{equation*}
