\begin{align*} f(x) \amp = 100 \cdot \left(1 + 0.05\right)^{4t} \qquad\amp \\ f(x) \amp = 100 \cdot \left(1.05\right)^{4t} \qquad\amp \\ f(x) \amp = 100 \cdot \Big(\left(1.05\right)^{4}\Big)^t \qquad\amp \text{Using }{\left(a^{r}\right)}^s= a^{r\cdot s}\\ f(x) \amp = 100 \cdot \Big(1.2155\Big)^t \qquad\amp \end{align*}

We have shown that \(f(t)\) is equal to \(a\cdot b^t\) with \(a=100\) and \(b=1.2155\text{.}\)
