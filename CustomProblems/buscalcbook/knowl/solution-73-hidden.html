Recall from Definition 3.13 that

\begin{equation*} \text{Profit} = \text{Revenue} - \text{Cost} \end{equation*}

That means to find \(P(x)\text{,}\) we must first find equations for \(R(x)\) and \(C(x)\text{.}\)

To find the equation for Revenue, recall from Definition 3.10 that

\begin{equation*} \text{Revenue} = \Big(\text{\# Items}\Big) \cdot \Big(\text{Item Cost}\Big) \end{equation*}

We write \(x\) for the number of items, and we have already found that the item price is \(p(x) = -0.1x + 21 \) in the scenerio where \(x\) items are sold. That means we just multiply \(x\) by \(p(x)\) to get revenue.

\begin{equation*} R(x) = \Big(x\Big) \cdot \Big(-0.1x + 21\Big) = -0.1x^2 + 21x \end{equation*}

To find the total cost, recall our approach from Exploration 2.8. If you are going to sell \(x\) items, you must pay a fixed cost of \(500\) dollars, as well as \(3\)$ for each item \(x\text{.}\) That gives us a total cost function

\begin{equation*} C(x) = 500 + 3x \end{equation*}

We can now combine the total revenue and total cost functions to get the profit function.

\begin{align*} \text{Profit} \amp = \text{Revenue} - \text{Cost} \\ P(x) \amp = R(x) - C(x) \\ \amp = ( -0.1x^2 + 21x) - (500 + 3x) \\ \amp = -0.1x^2 + 21x - 500 - 3x\\ P(x) \amp = -0.1x^2 + 18x - 500 \end{align*}
