Exploration 1.11. Changes and Formulas.

Suppose that on day \(x\text{,}\) the value of a certain stock is given by \(f(x) = x-0.1x^{2}\text{.}\)

(a)

Use the formula you are given to fill in the following table.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c} \text{day } (x) & 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 & 7 \\ \hline price \ (y) & \quad & \quad & \quad & \quad & \quad & \quad & \quad \\ \hline \text{change in price } (\Delta y) & & & & & & & \end{array} \end{equation*}

Hint: You can use the “Table” mode of a graphing calculator to speed this up significantly.

Solution.
(b)

Note that the change \(\Delta y = y_2 - y_1 = f(x_2) - f(x_1)\) implicitly depends on the distance \(\Delta x=x_2-x_1\text{.}\) Here, all our x values are consecutive, but you could imagine computing \(\Delta y\) (the change in \(y\)) in different increments, such as for \(x=1,3,5,7\text{.}\)
