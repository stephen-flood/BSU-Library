Suppose that \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) are differentiable functions. Then

\begin{equation*} \ddxfrac{\dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}} = \dfrac{g(x) \cdot \ddx{f(x)} - f(x) \cdot \ddx{g(x)} }{\Big(g(x)\Big)^2} \end{equation*}

It is often easier to memorize this formula in an abbreviated form:

  • \(\displaystyle \left[\dfrac{t}{b}\right]' = \dfrac{b\cdot t' - t\cdot b'}{(b)^2} \)

  • \(\displaystyle \left[\dfrac{\text{hi}}{\text{lo}} \right] = \dfrac{\text{lo }D\text{ hi} - \text{hi }D\text{ lo} }{(\text{lo})^2}\)
