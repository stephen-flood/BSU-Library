If you try to divide logarithms, such as in \(\dfrac{\ln(5)}{\ln(2)}\text{,}\) you will get something very different than our log laws. This is because of the surprising fact that

\begin{equation*} \log_b(x) = \dfrac{\ln(x)}{\ln(b)} \end{equation*}

For example, if you divide \(\dfrac{\ln(5)}{\ln(2)}\text{,}\) you actually get \(\dfrac{\ln(5)}{\ln(2)} = \log_2(5) = 0.9162\dots \text{,}\) which is not the same thing as \(\ln\left(\frac{2}{5} \right)=0.4306\dots\text{.}\)
