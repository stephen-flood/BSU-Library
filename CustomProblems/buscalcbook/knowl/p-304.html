We want to find the number of items \(x\) such that the total cost \(C(x)\) is equal to 300. In other words, we want to solve for \(x\) such that

\begin{equation*} 300 = C(x)\text{.} \end{equation*}

But we also know that \(C(x)=100+5x\text{,}\) so we can subsitute that equation for \(C(x)\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} 300 = 100 + 5x \end{equation*}

We can now solve this as normal

\begin{align*} 300 \amp =\amp 100 \amp + \amp 5x\\ -100\amp \amp -100 \amp \amp \\ \amp \\ 200 \amp =\amp \amp \amp 5x\\ \div 5 \amp\amp \amp \amp \div 5 \\ \amp \\ 40 \amp =\amp \amp \amp x \end{align*}

You are able to manufacture \(x=40\) items.
