Recopy the first logarithm \(\ln(10)\text{.}\) For the second logarithm, see that the input is a power, so write down the first law of logarithms \(\ln\Big( x^r \Big)= r\cdot \ln(x)\text{.}\) That gives

\begin{equation*} \ln(10\cdot 5^3) = \ln(10) + 3\cdot \ln( 5 )\text{.} \end{equation*}
